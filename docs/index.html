Theory

Mathematical Foundations

The rigorous mathematical models powering this platform

Black-Scholes-Merton Model
$$C = S_0 N(d_1) - K e^{-rT} N(d_2)$$ $$P = K e^{-rT} N(-d_2) - S_0 N(-d_1)$$
$$d_1 = \frac{\ln(S_0/K) + (r + \sigma^2/2)T}{\sigma\sqrt{T}}, \quad d_2 = d_1 - \sigma\sqrt{T}$$

Where $N(\cdot)$ is the standard normal CDF, $S_0$ is spot price, $K$ is strike, $r$ is risk-free rate, $\sigma$ is volatility, $T$ is time to expiry.

The Greeks
$$\Delta_C = N(d_1), \quad \Gamma = \frac{\phi(d_1)}{S\sigma\sqrt{T}}$$
$$\Theta_C = -\frac{S\phi(d_1)\sigma}{2\sqrt{T}} - rKe^{-rT}N(d_2)$$ $$\mathcal{V} = S\phi(d_1)\sqrt{T}, \quad \rho_C = KTe^{-rT}N(d_2)$$

Where $\phi(\cdot)$ is the standard normal PDF. Greeks measure sensitivity of option value to underlying risk factors.

Geometric Brownian Motion
$$dS = \mu S\,dt + \sigma S\,dW_t$$
$$S_T = S_0 \exp\!\left[\left(r - \tfrac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)T + \sigma\sqrt{T}\,Z\right], \quad Z \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$$

Risk-neutral pricing: simulate terminal prices, discount expected payoffs at the risk-free rate.

Value at Risk & Expected Shortfall
$$\text{VaR}_\alpha = -\inf\{x : P(L \le x) \ge \alpha\}$$
$$\text{ES}_\alpha = \frac{1}{1-\alpha}\int_\alpha^1 \text{VaR}_u\,du$$

VaR gives the maximum loss at confidence $\alpha$. ES (CVaR) is the expected loss given that VaR is exceeded — a coherent risk measure.

Binomial Tree (CRR)
$$u = e^{\sigma\sqrt{\Delta t}}, \quad d = 1/u, \quad p = \frac{e^{r\Delta t} - d}{u - d}$$
$$V_n = e^{-r\Delta t}\bigl[p\,V_{n+1}^u + (1\!-\!p)\,V_{n+1}^d\bigr]$$

Cox-Ross-Rubinstein lattice. Backward induction with optional early exercise check at each node for American options.

Put-Call Parity & Arbitrage
$$C - P = S_0 - K e^{-rT}$$
$$\varepsilon = |C - P - S_0 + Ke^{-rT}| \approx 0$$

Fundamental no-arbitrage relationship for European options. Any non-zero $\varepsilon$ implies arbitrage opportunity or pricing error.
